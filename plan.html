Future Plan

Where I'm Going

Human proposes, God disposes.

Short Term

I am a graduate assistant, and this internship was my way of testing whether QA was the right fit for me. It turned out to be more than a fit — I found something I genuinely love. In the short term, I want to keep building on what I learned here. I want to go deeper into test automation, sharpen my API testing skills, and eventually bring QA thinking into the academic spaces I already work in.

Long Term

I see myself educating people about what QA actually is — not just as a job title but as a discipline and a mindset. Whether that is in a classroom, a tech community, or both, I want to be someone who helps others understand why quality matters before users ever touch a product. Where exactly I will be, I honestly do not know yet. I am going with what feels right. Human proposes, God disposes.

What This Internship Taught Me

I came in thinking QA was about finding bugs. I leave knowing it is about communication, precision, and owning your mistakes. I failed three stages. I paid twice to stay in. I received feedback that stung and feedback that pushed me further than I thought I could go. Every piece of it made me better. This internship did not just teach me QA — it taught me how to keep going when things are hard, and how to be honest about what you do not know yet.
